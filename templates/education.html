📚 Menstrual Health Education

Learn about menstrual health, hygiene, nutrition, and myths.

🩸 What is Menstruation?

Menstruation is a natural biological process where the uterus sheds its lining once every month. This results in bleeding through the vagina and usually lasts between 3 to 7 days.

  • Occurs every 21–35 days in most women.
  • Part of the reproductive cycle.
  • Begins during puberty (around age 10–15).
  • Stops during menopause (around age 45–50).

🧼 Menstrual Hygiene

Maintaining proper hygiene during menstruation is essential to prevent infections and ensure comfort.

  • Use sanitary pads, menstrual cups, or tampons.
  • Change pads every 4–6 hours.
  • Wash hands before and after changing products.
  • Dispose of sanitary products properly.
  • Keep the genital area clean and dry.

🥗 Nutrition During Periods

Eating a balanced diet helps reduce fatigue and supports the body during menstruation.

  • Eat iron-rich foods like spinach and lentils.
  • Include fruits such as bananas and oranges.
  • Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.
  • Avoid excessive caffeine and junk food.
  • Consume nuts and seeds for healthy fats.

⚠️ Common Symptoms

Many people experience physical and emotional symptoms during periods. These symptoms are normal but vary from person to person.

  • Abdominal cramps
  • Back pain
  • Mood swings
  • Fatigue
  • Headaches

Light exercise, warm compresses, and proper rest can help relieve symptoms.

❌ Myths vs Facts

  • Myth: Women should not bathe during periods.
    Fact: Bathing helps maintain hygiene and relieve cramps.
  • Myth: Period blood is dirty.
    Fact: It is a natural mixture of blood and tissue.
  • Myth: Exercise should be avoided.
    Fact: Light exercise can reduce cramps and improve mood.

👩‍⚕️ When to See a Doctor

While periods are normal, some symptoms require medical attention.

  • Extremely painful cramps
  • Periods lasting more than 7 days
  • Very heavy bleeding
  • Missing periods frequently
  • Severe dizziness or weakness
